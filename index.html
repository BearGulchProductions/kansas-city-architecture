More Than Meets the Eye

Kansas City is a city that rewards looking up. Its south side opens onto one of the most ambitious commercial districts in America, styled after the grandeur of Seville, Spain. Its boulevard system — one of the finest in the country — unfolds in miles of tree-lined parkways punctuated by more than 200 public fountains. Behind every building is a history: of political machines and civic boosters, of a 1920s bond issue that made a Depression-era building boom possible.

This guide is for everyone — the newcomer arriving for the World Cup, the lifelong resident who has never stopped to read the building across the street, the architecture student, the history reader, the curious walker. Look up. There is a lot to see.
