About This Site

Look Up, Kansas City is an architecture and history guide to one of America's most underappreciated cities. It is not a tourism brochure, a directory, or a Wikipedia mirror. It is an attempt to explain Kansas City through its buildings — what was built, when, by whom, and why it still matters.

The site was built by a Kansas City local with a deep affection for the city and a conviction that its architectural story deserves to be told in full. Kansas City has a Beaux Arts train station that rivals Penn Station, an Art Deco skyline of extraordinary coherence, a concert hall by one of the world's great living architects, and more public fountains per capita than any city except Rome. Most visitors drive past all of it without knowing what they're looking at. This site is for the ones who want to look up.

All photography is sourced from Wikimedia Commons under public domain and Creative Commons licenses. A full credits page is linked below. The writing attempts to be honest — about what is beautiful, about what was lost, and about the complicated people and politics behind the buildings that remain.
